# Module 3: Environment Awareness & Decontamination

**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Required Score:** 100% for completion

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:
- Identify high-toxicity zones using drone reconnaissance
- Execute proper decontamination procedures
- Recognize symptoms of exposure and report them correctly

## 3.1 Toxin Identification

### Drone-Based Hazard Mapping
- **Thermal Anomalies:** Temperature differences >5°C from ambient indicate chemical seepage
- **Water Discoloration:** Green, purple, or metallic sheen zones marked as HIGH RISK
- **Air Quality Sensors:** Areas with VOC readings >500ppm require respirator upgrade

### Ground-Level Indicators
- **Dead Vegetation:** 20-meter radius around dead plants indicates chemical runoff
- **Foam/Slick:** Surface foam formation signals petroleum contamination
- **Odor:** Sulfur or ammonia smells indicate active chemical processes

## 3.2 Decontamination Procedures

### Field Decontamination Station
Located at every drop-hub with:
- 10% bleach solution spray bottles
- High-pressure freshwater rinse (minimum 50 PSI)
- PPE removal station with color-coded zones

### Decon Protocol
1. **Outer Rinse:** All equipment rinsed with freshwater before approaching hub
2. **PPE Removal:** Remove items in order: gloves → boots → coveralls → respirator
3. **Body Wash:** 5-minute wash with bleach solution, scrub all exposed skin
4. **Final Inspection:** Guardian signs off on cleanliness before leaving site

## 3.3 Exposure Reporting

### Immediate Symptoms
- Skin irritation, rash, or burns
- Difficulty breathing or chest tightness
- Headache, dizziness, or nausea
- Eye irritation or vision changes

### Reporting Chain
1. Notify immediate supervisor via radio (Channel 1)
2. Document symptoms in incident log with timestamp
3. Seek medical attention at designated clinic
4. Equipment quarantined pending investigation
